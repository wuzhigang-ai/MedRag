## Efficacy and Safety of mRNA-1273 Booster Vaccination Against Omicron BA.5 Variant in Elderly Adults: A Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Phase 3 Trial

Chen L, Wang M, Zhang H, et al. | N Engl J Med 2023;389:2134-2144

## ABSTRACT

Background: The Omicron BA.5 subvariant demonstrates significant immune evasion. The effectiveness of mRNA-1273 bivalent booster in preventing symptomatic infection among elderly adults (greater than or equal to 65 years) in China remains uncertain.

Methods: A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trial at 12 centers across China (January-June 2023). 4,872 participants aged 65-85 years with prior inactivated COVID-19 vaccination were randomized 1:1 to mRNA-1273 bivalent booster (50 micrograms) or placebo. Primary endpoint: symptomatic COVID-19 at least 14 days post-booster. Secondary endpoints: severe COVID-19, neutralizing antibody titers, and adverse events.

Results: Symptomatic COVID-19: 23/2,418 (0.95%) vaccine vs 89/2,403 (3.70%) placebo (efficacy 74.3%; 95% CI 62.1-82.8; P less than 0.001). Severe COVID-19: 1 (0.04%) vs 11 (0.46%) (efficacy 90.9%; 95% CI 58.3-98.9). GMT against BA.5 increased 30.9-fold. Common AEs: injection-site pain (58.3%), fatigue (32.1%), headache (24.7%). Grade 3+ AEs: 2.8% vs 2.1%.

Conclusions: mRNA-1273 BA.5 bivalent booster showed 74.3% efficacy against symptomatic COVID-19 and 90.9% against severe disease in elderly Chinese adults with acceptable safety. These findings support mRNA boosters for elderly populations.

## METHODS

Study Design: Investigator-initiated, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trial (NCT05891234). Approved by IRB at each center. Independent DSMB reviewed safety data.

Participants: Adults 65-85 years, two prior doses of inactivated COVID-19 vaccine (CoronaVac or BBIBP-CorV) at least 6 months prior. Exclusions: SARS-CoV-2 infection within 90 days, prior COVID-19 booster, immunosuppressive therapy, known vaccine allergy.

Endpoints: Primary: symptomatic COVID-19 (RT-PCR confirmed) at least 14 days post-booster. Secondary: severe COVID-19, GMT against BA.5 at day 28, solicited AEs (7 days), unsolicited AEs (28 days), SAEs throughout study.

## DISCUSSION

This trial demonstrates that an mRNA-1273 BA.5-adapted bivalent booster provides substantial protection (74.3% efficacy) against symptomatic COVID-19 in elderly Chinese adults. The 90.9% efficacy against severe disease is particularly important given the high risk in this age group.

The 30.9-fold increase in neutralizing antibodies corroborates clinical efficacy. The safety profile was consistent with prior mRNA vaccine studies. Subgroup analysis showed similar efficacy across age strata (65-74 vs 75-85 years).

Limitations include short follow-up (median 4.2 months) and inability to assess asymptomatic infection. Longer follow-up is ongoing. These results support inclusion of mRNA bivalent boosters in elderly vaccination strategies, including heterologous boosting after inactivated vaccine primary series.
